Tip 01

Set your writing style first

Paste a sample of your real writing before humanizing. Even 5 sentences from an email or text gives the AI enough to pick up your exact rhythm and vocabulary. It's the single biggest improvement you can make.

Tip 02

Pick depth based on your draft

Balanced mode is good for light clean-ups. Deep revision mode does more sentence-level work while preserving your facts and intent. Use it when the draft is vague, repetitive, or hard to follow.

Tip 03

Make 2–3 edits yourself

After humanizing, change 1–2 sentences to exactly how you'd say them. This injects genuinely unpredictable patterns that no AI rewriter can produce. Takes 30 seconds and makes a real difference.

Tip 04

Split long texts

For anything over 700 words, split into 2–3 sections and humanize each separately. The model focuses better on shorter chunks and the output quality is noticeably better on longer texts.

Tip 05

Run a second pass when needed

Revise → copy output → paste it back → run on Subtle mode. The second pass can tighten transitions and remove leftover wordiness without changing your core message.

Tip 06

Check rhythm and specificity metrics

After revising you'll see edit ratio, specificity, repetition, sentence-length variance, and voice similarity. If specificity drops or voice similarity falls, revise again.

Tip 07

How the datasets work

Every run can use optional reference examples to improve clarity and specificity checks. These references are guardrails for quality, not a target style to imitate.

Tip 08

Quiz answers are hard rules

When you answer the style quiz, your answers become absolute rules enforced separately from the rewrite instructions. If you said never use "And" to start a sentence, that gets checked against every sentence in the output before it's returned.
